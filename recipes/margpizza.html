Margherita Pizza

Description

Everyone loves pizza and this one is no exception! This super simple pie is a classic that will sure to please any pizza parties you plan to host. The key to this pizza are the ingredients. Try and get a hold of local tomatoes that are in season. The summer is a great time, as tomatoes are unbelievably juicy and sweet. Fresh basil from your community farmers market is ideal. Basil brings so much fregrance and a subtle harbacious quality that cannot be replicated. Lastly, splurge a little. Buy yourself high-quality mozzarella. The mozzarella is what brings everything together. Finish it off with a drizzle of grassy Extra Virgin Olive Oil; it just puts it over the top. Super savory, crispy and light, all while being so comforting and satisfying to eat!

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 1 cup + 1 tbsp (153 g) 00 Flour
  • 1 cup + 1 tbsp (153 g) All Purpose Flour
  • Kosher Salt
  • 3/4 (2 g) tsp Active Dry Yeast
  • 1 tsp (4 g) Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Toppings

  • 2 Medium Heirloom Tomatoes
  • 2 Medium Mozzarella Balls
  • 16 Basil Leaves
  • 2-3 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Show me the dough...

  1. In a large bowl, combine both flours and add two large pinches of salt.
  2. Incorporate a little less than a cup of water (200 g), olive oil, and yeast in with the flour.
  3. Using your hands or a stand mixer, knead dough unitl well combined, about 3-5minutes.
  4. Let dough rest for at least 15 minutes. You can let the dough rest overnight in the fridge if you wanted.

Makin' it rain (with toppings)...

  1. Slice tomatoes 1/4 inch thick, sprinkle with a small pinch of salt, and set aside.
  2. Slice a 1/4 inch thick or pull apart the mozzarella into bite size strands.
  3. Stack the basil on top of each other and roll them titghtly. Slice the basil roll to create thin ribbons. You can reserve a few leaves for garnish, but of course optional.

It tastes like a million bucks...

  1. After the dough has rested, generously flour your hands and surface.
  2. Knead your dough and roll it out until it is about 12-14 inches round and 1/4 inch thick.
  3. Brush your dough with a bit of Extra Virgin Olive Oil and start laying out your toppings.
  4. Researve half the basil.
  5. Bake at 500F for 15-20 minutes, until the mozzarella has some golden spots and the tomatoes have soften.
  6. Finish with a final drizzle of Extra Virgin Olive Oil and garnish with your fresh basil.
  7. Enjoy!
